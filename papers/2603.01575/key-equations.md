
# Key Equations and Formal Structure
arXiv:2603.01575  
*Intersubjectivity as a principle determining physical observables and non-classicality*

---

## Observable Definition

The paper proposes that physical observables emerge through **intersubjective agreement** between observers.

Conceptually:

observable = shared measurement structure

This shifts the definition of observables from purely objective quantities to quantities stabilized through consistent measurement relationships.

---

## Measurement Consistency

Observables must satisfy reproducibility across observers:

O_i(state) ≈ O_j(state)

where

- O_i = observable measured by observer i  
- O_j = observable measured by observer j  

Agreement across observers determines whether a quantity qualifies as a physical observable.

---

## Non-Classicality Condition

Non-classical behavior arises when measurement correlations cannot be reduced to classical joint distributions.

Formally related to:

P(a,b | A,B) ≠ Σ_λ P(a|A,λ) P(b|B,λ) P(λ)

This expresses the standard Bell-type separation between classical and non-classical correlations.

## Predictive Structure

The framework connects to predictive processing models.

A simplified representation:

prediction_error = observation − prediction

Systems adjust internal models to minimize prediction error across interacting observers.

## Interpretation

The paper proposes:

physical reality → stabilized through shared observation

rather than assuming observables exist independently of measurement contexts.
