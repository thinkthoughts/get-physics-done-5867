# arXiv:2603.01575

**Title**  
Intersubjectivity as a principle determining physical observables and non-classicality

**Authors**  
Shun Umekawa, Koki Ono, Hayato Arai

**Topic**  
Physical observables, non-classicality, predictive processing, intersubjectivity

## Summary

This paper proposes that physical observables are not best understood as fully isolated, purely objective quantities. Instead, observables become meaningful through **intersubjective agreement**: stable relationships among observers, measurements, and shared interpretive structures.

The authors connect this idea to broader frameworks in cognition and science, arguing that observables emerge through coordinated measurement practices rather than from detached description alone. In that sense, the paper links physical measurement to relational structure, predictive processing, and the conditions under which non-classical behavior becomes identifiable.

## Why It Matters

The paper offers a conceptual bridge between:

- quantum measurement and non-classical correlations
- observer agreement and measurement stability
- predictive processing and physical interpretation

This makes it relevant to questions about how observables are defined, how measurement contexts matter, and how shared structure shapes what counts as physical reality.
